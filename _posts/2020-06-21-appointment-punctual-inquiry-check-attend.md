## 1. Appointment, Rendezvous, Meeting

Cả 3 từ trên đều mang tiếng việt là cuộc gặp, cuộc hẹn. Nhưng chúng cõ những sự khác biệt nho nhỏ.

**Appointment**: a meeting for professional service = arrangement (more formal)

Ex: *What time is your appointment with the dentist?*

Không dùng *rendezvous* và *meeting*

**Rendezvous**: a meeting, often secret or in strange circumstances.

Ex: *The directors had a secret rendezvous before the formal meeting took place.*

## 2. Punctual and On Time

Cả hai từ trên đều mang nghĩa là đúng giờ = *not late*.

**Punctual** is more formal than **on time**.

In addition, *punctual* is used to describe a person who is always on time. **On time** is a state to be in.

Ex: *He is punctual.*

## 3. Inquiry, Question, Query

Cả 3 từ trên mang nghĩa gần giống nhau. Chúng khác nhau về độ formal: **Inquiry** > **Question** > **Query**. 

*I have an inquiry*: in a more formal stuation.

*I have a question*: in school and with friend.

*I have a query*: is rarely used. 


## 4. Urgency and Emergency

Cả 2 đều mang nghĩa tiếng việt là khẩn cấp.

**Urgency**: just important.

Ex: *She didn't understand the urgency of the situation.*

**Emergency**: dangerous and serious > *urgency*.

Ex: *You can land a plane on water in an emergency.*

## 5. Check vs Test

Cả hai từ đều mang nghĩa là kiểm tra.

**Check**: kiểm tra để xác nhận. = *Examine*

Ex: *The doctor will call next week to check on your progress.*

**Test**: kiểm tra để thử, khám phá. = *Alalyze*

Ex: *The manufacturers are currently testing the new engine.*

## 6. Join in, Attend, Participate in, Take part in

Chúng kều mang nghĩa là tham gia, tham dự.

**Join in**: tham gia vào một hoạt động đang diễn ra, tiến hành.

Ex: *Everyone can join in when I sing the National Anthem.*

**Attend**: tham dự/ tham gia với tính trang trọng.

Ex: *The meeting is on the fifth and we're hoping everyone will attend.*

**Participate in, Take part in**: tham dự/ tham gia với ít trang trọng hơn.

Ex: *She never participates in any of our discussions.*
All the children took part in the Thanksgiving play.

## 7. On duty and Off duty

**On duty**: working, and responsible for dealing with things that happen at work.

Ex: *During office hours there is always a planning officer on duty who deals with enquiries.*

**Off duty**: not working, and not responsible for dealing with things that happen at work.

Ex: *When your Estate Manager goes off duty, the alarm system will be monitored by a remote service.*
